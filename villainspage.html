Who opposes the Batman?

The Joker: The Joker is arguably Batman's most iconic and enduring adversary. He first appeared in "Batman #1" in 1940. The Joker is a psychopathic criminal mastermind with a sadistic sense of humor.
His origin story is often portrayed as ambiguous, with different versions suggesting various pasts, including falling into a vat of chemicals that bleach his skin and turn his hair green.
The Joker is obsessed with chaos and causing mayhem in Gotham City, often engaging in elaborate schemes to challenge Batman's moral code and push him to his limits.

Two-Face (Harvey Dent): Harvey Dent was once Gotham City's idealistic district attorney, known for his crusade against crime and corruption.
However, after half of his face is scarred by acid during a courtroom trial gone wrong, Dent suffers from severe mental trauma and develops a split personality.
He becomes the villain Two-Face, flipping a coin to make decisions and embracing the duality of chance and fate.
Two-Face is obsessed with the concept of fairness and often commits crimes based on the outcome of his coin toss, serving as a dark reflection of Batman's own struggle with justice and vengeance.

Catwoman (Selina Kyle): Catwoman is a complex character who blurs the line between villain and antihero. Selina Kyle is a skilled cat burglar with a complicated relationship with Batman.
While she often operates outside the law, she has a strong moral code and frequently shows compassion for the less fortunate. Catwoman is known for her agility, whip proficiency, and affinity for cats.
Her backstory varies across different interpretations, but she is typically portrayed as a woman with a troubled past who uses her skills to survive in Gotham's criminal underworld.
Despite her criminal activities, Catwoman has a romantic connection with Batman, leading to a complex dynamic between them as adversaries, allies, and sometimes even lovers.
